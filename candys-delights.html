My Story

Hello, I'm Candy

I fell in love with baking at seven years old, perched on a stool in my grandmother's kitchen, watching her fold red bean paste into paper-thin mochi wrappers with impossible grace. She never measured anything — everything was by feel, by smell, by intuition.

That kitchen became my first classroom. I spent years recreating her recipes, then branching out into the French patisserie techniques I studied in culinary school, and eventually finding my own voice — a fusion of East and West, tradition and experimentation.

Candy's Delights began as a weekend blog where I shared my recipe notes. What I didn't expect was the community it would become — thousands of home bakers who write to tell me how Candy's mochi became a family staple, or how my cookie recipe made someone's terrible Tuesday a little sweeter.

That's why I keep baking. That's why I keep sharing.

Coconut Mochi

Pillowy, chewy steamed mochi filled with coconut flakes, peanut candy, roasted sesame, and Skor — wrapped in shredded coconut for a truly indulgent bite.

🍡

About This Recipe

This coconut mochi is a labour of love — and one of the most satisfying things to make in my kitchen. The outer dough is steamed rather than microwaved, giving it a deeply chewy, springy texture that's worlds apart from store-bought mochi. Coconut milk runs through the whole thing, making it fragrant and rich.

The filling is what makes this truly special. A combination of toasted coconut flakes, crunchy peanut candy, nutty roasted sesame, and the buttery toffee crunch of Skor creates a sweet, textured interior that surprises you with every bite. Each piece is then rolled in shredded coconut, which adds another layer of tropical flavour and a beautiful snowy finish.

This recipe takes about an hour of steaming, but the active work is minimal. Make it on a weekend afternoon, and you'll have a stunning box of mochi ready to share — or keep all to yourself. No judgment here.

20
Min Prep
~16
Pieces
★★★
Easy
60
Min Steam

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Mix the Mochi Dough

In a large bowl, combine 380g rice flour, 70g wheat starch, 300g sugar, one 400g can of full-fat coconut milk, 300g water, 70g oil, and 2 drops of food colouring (if using). Whisk until completely smooth with no lumps — the batter should be pourable and uniform in colour.

2

Steam the Dough

Pour the batter into a greased, heatproof tray or pan. Cover tightly with aluminium foil — this is important to prevent condensation dripping in and making the dough watery. Steam over high heat for 60 minutes. The mochi is done when it is set, glossy, and slightly translucent. Let it cool enough to handle.

3

Prepare the Filling

While the mochi steams, prepare your filling station: toast the coconut flakes lightly in a dry pan until golden. Roughly crush the peanut candy into small pieces. Mix together the coconut flakes, peanut candy pieces, roasted white sesame seeds, and crushed Skor in a bowl. Set aside.

4

Portion the Dough

Dust a clean work surface generously with shredded coconut. Portion the mochi dough into 45g pieces. Work quickly — the dough is easiest to shape when warm. Keep your hands lightly oiled or dusted to prevent sticking.

5

Fill & Wrap

Flatten each 45g piece into a round disc. Place a generous spoonful of the filling mixture in the centre. Gather the edges of the dough up and around the filling, pinching firmly to seal. Roll gently between your palms to form a smooth ball, then roll in shredded coconut to coat completely.

6

Set & Serve

Place completed mochi on a coconut-dusted tray. They can be served at room temperature or lightly chilled. Best eaten the day they are made, when the mochi is at its softest and the filling is still crunchy.

💡 Candy's TipsWork quickly once the mochi is cool enough to handle — it becomes stiffer as it cools further. If it tightens up, a brief moment back over the steamer will soften it again. Make sure your foil seal is tight during steaming to get the right texture. The Skor adds a butterscotch crunch that's completely addictive — don't skip it!

🛒 Outer Dough

  • Rice flour380g
  • Wheat starch70g
  • Sugar300g
  • Coconut milk (1 can)400g
  • Water300g
  • Oil70g
  • Food colouring2 drops

🍬 Filling

  • Coconut flakesto taste
  • Peanut candyto taste
  • Roasted white sesameto taste
  • Skor (toffee bits)to taste
  • Shredded coconut (coating)generous

📋 Quick Notes

StorageKeep in an airtight container at room temperature for 1 day, or in the fridge for up to 2 days. Do not freeze.

DietaryNaturally dairy-free. Check your peanut candy and Skor labels for allergen info.

The Perfect Cookies

Loaded with oats, coconut, cranberries, blueberries, chocolate, toffee bits, and almonds — one cookie has it all.

🍪

About This Recipe

Some cookies are about one thing — chocolate, or caramel, or a single perfect ingredient shining through. These cookies are about everything. Oats for chew, coconut for richness, cranberries and blueberries for a pop of fruit, sliced almonds for crunch, chocolate chips for indulgence, and SKOR toffee bits for that irresistible buttery crunch.

The dough comes together quickly in one bowl — no chilling required — and the cookies bake in just 10 minutes. The key is pulling them while the centres still look slightly underdone. They'll continue to set on the pan, giving you that perfect chewy centre with golden, crispy edges.

Finish each one with a whole almond pressed on top before baking. It's a small touch that makes them look as good as they taste.

15
Min Prep
16–20
Cookies
★★★
Easy
10
Min Bake

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Preheat & Prep

Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C). Line two large baking trays with parchment paper and set aside.

2

Cream Butter & Sugars

In a large bowl, beat together ½ cup softened butter, ¼ cup packed brown sugar, and ¼ cup granulated sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in 1 egg, then add 1 tbsp milk and 1 tsp vanilla and mix until smooth.

3

Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a separate bowl, whisk together 1 cup all-purpose flour, ½ tsp baking powder, ½ tsp baking soda, and a pinch of salt. Stir in 1 cup large-flake rolled oats. Add the flour-oat mixture to the butter mixture and stir until just combined.

4

Fold in the Good Stuff

Stir in ½ cup shredded coconut, ½ cup sliced almonds, ½ cup dried cranberries, ½ cup dried blueberries, ½ cup semi-sweet chocolate chips, ¼ cup SKOR toffee bits, and 2 tbsp flaxseeds. The dough will be thick and gloriously loaded.

5

Scoop & Decorate

Use an ice-cream scoop to portion the dough onto the prepared trays, spacing each about 2 inches (5 cm) apart. Slightly press and flatten each ball. Press one whole almond into the top of each cookie.

6

Bake & Cool

Bake for 10 minutes, until the edges are golden but the centres still look slightly underdone — this is correct. Let cool on the pans for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack and cool completely. They firm up beautifully as they cool.

💡 Candy's TipsDon't overbake these — the 10-minute mark is your friend. Pulling them early is the secret to that chewy, bakery-style texture. Room-temperature butter creams much more smoothly than cold butter, so set it out 30 minutes ahead. The toffee bits are non-negotiable — they melt into little pockets of caramel heaven.

🛒 Ingredients

  • Butter, softened½ cup
  • Brown sugar, packed¼ cup
  • Granulated sugar¼ cup
  • Egg1
  • Milk1 tbsp
  • Vanilla extract1 tsp
  • All-purpose flour1 cup
  • Large-flake rolled oats1 cup
  • Baking powder½ tsp
  • Baking soda½ tsp
  • Saltpinch
  • Large shredded coconut½ cup
  • Sliced almonds½ cup
  • Dried cranberries½ cup
  • Dried blueberries½ cup
  • Semi-sweet chocolate chips½ cup
  • SKOR toffee bits¼ cup
  • Flaxseeds2 tbsp
  • Whole almonds (for décor)16–20

📋 Quick Notes

StorageStore cooled cookies in an airtight container at room temp for 5 days. Freeze baked cookies or dough balls for up to 3 months.

Swap the Mix-InsSwap dried blueberries for raisins, or use pecans instead of almonds. White chocolate chips work beautifully in place of semi-sweet.

Oven TipEvery oven runs differently — start checking at 9 minutes. The cookies should look underdone in the centre; they firm up as they cool.

Banana Cupcakes

Moist, warmly spiced banana cupcakes loaded with an incredible crunchy topping — coconut, chocolate, cranberries, almonds, and more.

🧁

About This Recipe

These banana cupcakes are everything you want from a banana bake — deeply moist, warmly spiced with cinnamon, and carrying that irresistible ripe banana sweetness. The sour cream keeps the crumb tender and adds a subtle tang that balances the sweetness beautifully.

But the real magic is the topping. Instead of frosting, these cupcakes get an extraordinary crown of coconut flakes, chocolate chips, dried cranberries, flax seed, and sliced almonds — all pressed gently on top of the batter before baking. It toasts as the cupcakes bake, creating a crunchy, nutty, chocolatey crust that is genuinely addictive.

The riper your bananas, the better. Look for ones with black-speckled skin — they're sweeter, softer, and pack far more banana flavour into the batter.

15
Min Prep
12
Cupcakes
★★★
Easy
30
Min Bake

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Preheat & Prep

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a 12-hole muffin tin with paper cupcake cases or grease well. Peel 3 very ripe bananas and mash them thoroughly in a large bowl until smooth with only a few small lumps remaining.

2

Mix the Wet Ingredients

To the mashed bananas, add ¾ cup sugar, ⅓ cup oil, 2 eggs, ½ cup sour cream, and 1 teaspoon vanilla extract. Whisk everything together until well combined and smooth.

3

Combine the Dry Ingredients

In a separate bowl, whisk together 1½ cups all-purpose flour, 1 tablespoon cinnamon, 1 teaspoon baking soda, and ¼ teaspoon salt. The generous amount of cinnamon is intentional — it pairs beautifully with banana and makes the whole kitchen smell incredible.

4

Fold Together

Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and fold gently with a spatula until just combined — a few streaks of flour are fine. Do not overmix, as this will make the cupcakes tough.

5

Fill & Top

Divide the batter evenly between the 12 cupcake cases, filling each about ¾ full. Generously scatter the topping over each cupcake — ¼ cup coconut flakes, ½ cup chocolate chips, ½ cup dried cranberries, 2 tbsp flax seed, 1 tbsp Skor, and ½ cup sliced almonds, divided between all 12. Press lightly so the toppings adhere.

6

Bake

Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 30 minutes, until a skewer inserted in the centre comes out clean and the tops are golden. The coconut and almonds should be toasted and fragrant. Let cool in the tin for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack.

💡 Candy's TipsUse the blackest, most over-ripe bananas you can find — they make the most flavourful batter. The topping combination is generous by design; the different textures and flavours all bake together into something magical. Don't be shy with it.

🛒 Wet Ingredients

  • Very ripe bananas3
  • Sugar¾ cup
  • Oil⅓ cup
  • Eggs2
  • Sour cream½ cup
  • Vanilla extract1 tsp

🌾 Dry Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour1½ cups
  • Cinnamon1 tbsp
  • Baking soda1 tsp
  • Salt¼ tsp

🍫 Topping

  • Coconut flakes¼ cup
  • Chocolate chips½ cup
  • Dried cranberries½ cup
  • Flax seed2 tbsp
  • Skor (toffee bits)1 tbsp
  • Sliced almonds½ cup
  • Walnuts or extra choc chipsoptional

📋 Quick Notes

StorageStore in an airtight container at room temp for 3 days, or freeze individually for up to 2 months.

No Sour Cream?Full-fat Greek yoghurt is a perfect 1:1 substitute.

Mango Pudding

Silky, bright, and bursting with mango — this simple gelatin pudding with whipping cream and a squeeze of lemon is pure tropical comfort.

🍮

About This Recipe

Mango pudding holds a very special place in my heart. Every trip to yum cha (dim sum) with my family ended the same way — a wobbling pot of golden mango pudding shared between everyone at the table. It was always the highlight.

This version uses canned mango purée for consistent, intense flavour year-round, combined with whipping cream for richness and small chunks of fresh mango folded in for texture. A squeeze of lemon at the end brightens the whole thing and makes the mango flavour really sing.

The method is simple: bloom your gelatin, dissolve sugar in water, bring it all together, then chill. The hardest part really is waiting. Make it the night before a dinner party and you'll be rewarded with the most impressive, effortless dessert on the table.

20
Min Active
6
Servings
★★★
Easy
4h+
Chill Time

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Bloom the Gelatin

In a small bowl, sprinkle 6 tablespoons of gelatin powder over 1 cup of cold water. Stir briefly, then let sit for 5–10 minutes without disturbing until it becomes spongy and swollen. This blooming step ensures the gelatin will dissolve completely without any lumps.

2

Make the Sugar Syrup

In a medium saucepan, combine 4 cups of water with 1 cup of sugar. Bring to a gentle heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves completely. Do not boil vigorously. Once the sugar is fully dissolved, reduce the heat to low.

3

Dissolve the Gelatin

Add the bloomed gelatin to the warm sugar syrup and stir gently until completely dissolved and the liquid is clear with no lumps. Remove from heat and allow to cool for a few minutes — it should be warm but not steaming hot before you add the other ingredients.

4

Add Mango Purée

Stir in the full can of mango purée until completely combined. The mixture should turn a vivid golden-orange. Taste at this point — it should be intensely mango-flavoured and well-sweetened. Adjust sugar if your purée is less sweet.

5

Fold in Mango Bits & Cream

Stir in 1 cup of whipping cream and gently fold in small diced fresh mango pieces for texture. Mix until uniform. Finally, squeeze in the juice of half a lemon and stir — this is the secret finishing touch that lifts and brightens the whole pudding.

6

Pour, Set & Serve

Pour the mixture through a fine sieve into individual ramekins, glasses, or moulds. Tap gently on the counter to release air bubbles. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for a minimum of 4 hours, or overnight. Serve straight in the ramekins or unmould onto plates for a classic dome presentation.

💡 Candy's TipsThe order matters here: water → sugar → gelatin → mango purée → mango pieces → whipping cream → lemon. Adding the cream and lemon last keeps the pudding from curdling. Don't skip the lemon — even a small amount makes the flavour dramatically brighter. Use the best quality canned mango purée you can find; Alphonso mango purée is exceptional.

🛒 Ingredients

  • Canned mango purée1 can
  • Whipping cream1 cup
  • Gelatin powder6 tbsp
  • Fresh mango (diced small)to taste
  • Water (for gelatin)1 cup
  • Water (for syrup)4 cups
  • Sugar1 cup
  • Lemon½ (juiced)

📋 Quick Notes

StorageKeep covered in the fridge for up to 3 days. The texture is best in the first 48 hours.

Make-AheadPerfect for dinner parties — make the day before and refrigerate overnight for best results.

Serving TipServe cold, drizzled with a little extra whipping cream or condensed milk on top.
