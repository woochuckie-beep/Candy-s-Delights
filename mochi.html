Strawberry Mochi

Pillowy soft rice cakes wrapped around a luscious strawberry centre — a Japanese classic made approachable for every home kitchen.

🍡

About This Recipe

Mochi is one of my all-time favourite things to make. There's something almost meditative about the process — the sticky dough transforms into the most pillowy, delicate wrapper you've ever tasted. This recipe is inspired by ichigo daifuku, a Japanese confection of sweet rice cake encasing a fresh strawberry in red bean paste.

My version simplifies the process without sacrificing that silky, chewy texture. I've tested this dozens of times to get the ratios just right, and the secret is in the balance of glutinous rice flour, water ratio, and a generous coating of potato starch (or cornstarch) to keep everything from sticking.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Prepare the Strawberries

Wash and hull 12 small strawberries. Pat completely dry with paper towels — any moisture will make the mochi wet. Wrap each strawberry with approximately 1 tablespoon of sweet red bean paste (anko), shaping it around the fruit so the tip of the strawberry peeks through. Place on a lined tray and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

2

Make the Mochi Dough

In a large microwave-safe bowl, whisk together 200g glutinous rice flour (mochiko), 180g sugar, and 280ml water until completely smooth with no lumps. Cover loosely with plastic wrap and microwave on high for 2 minutes. Stir well, then microwave again for 1–2 minutes until the dough looks glossy and translucent. It should be thick and very sticky.

3

Colour & Cool

If using food colouring, knead it in now while the dough is warm (use gloves — it stains!). Dust a large surface generously with potato starch or cornstarch. Carefully turn the hot dough out onto the surface and dust the top too. Let it cool for 10–15 minutes until it's warm but handleable.

4

Divide the Dough

Using a well-dusted rolling pin, roll the dough to about 5mm thickness. Using a round cutter or glass (about 9cm diameter), cut 12 circles. Dust each piece well so they don't stick to each other. Work quickly — mochi gets less pliable as it cools.

5

Wrap the Filling

Place a chilled anko-wrapped strawberry in the centre of a mochi circle (strawberry tip pointing down). Gather the edges of the dough up and around the filling, pinching them together firmly at the top to seal. Dust with starch and place seam-side down. Repeat with remaining mochi and strawberries.

6

Chill & Serve

Place completed mochi on a starch-dusted tray, cover lightly, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving. They're best eaten within 24 hours when the mochi is at its softest. Serve chilled and enjoy within 2 days.

💡 Candy's Tips Work quickly once you start wrapping — mochi hardens as it cools. If it becomes too stiff, microwave for 10–15 seconds to soften. Keep everything generously dusted with starch to prevent sticking. Damp hands can also help when pinching the seams.

📋 Quick Notes

Storage Keep in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days. Do not freeze.

Substitute No anko? Use Nutella, lotus paste, or white bean paste instead.

Dietary Naturally vegan and gluten-free. Always check your anko brand.
