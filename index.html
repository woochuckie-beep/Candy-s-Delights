Coconut Mochi

Pillowy, chewy steamed mochi filled with coconut flakes, peanut candy, roasted sesame, and Skor — wrapped in shredded coconut for a truly indulgent bite.

About This Recipe

This coconut mochi is a labour of love — and one of the most satisfying things to make in my kitchen. The outer dough is steamed rather than microwaved, giving it a deeply chewy, silky texture that stores beautifully.

The filling is what makes this truly special. A combination of toasted coconut flakes, crunchy peanut candy, nutty roasted sesame, and the buttery toffee crunch of Skor creates a sweet symphony in every bite.

This recipe takes about an hour of steaming, but the active work is minimal. Make it on a weekend afternoon, and you'll have a stunning box of mochi ready to share — or keep all to yourself.

20
Min Prep
~16
Pieces
★★★
Easy
60
Min Steam

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Mix the Mochi Dough

In a large bowl, combine 380g rice flour, 70g wheat starch, 300g sugar, one 400g can of full-fat coconut milk, 300g water, 70g oil, and 2 drops of food colouring (if using). Whisk until smooth and no lumps remain.

2

Steam the Dough

Pour the batter into a greased, heatproof tray or pan. Cover tightly with aluminium foil — this is important to prevent condensation dripping in and making the dough watery. Steam for 60 minutes until the dough is completely cooked through and translucent.

3

Prepare the Filling

While the mochi steams, prepare your filling station: toast the coconut flakes lightly in a dry pan until golden. Roughly crush the peanut candy into small pieces. Mix together the toasted coconut, peanut candy, roasted sesame, and Skor bits in a bowl.

4

Portion the Dough

Dust a clean work surface generously with shredded coconut (this acts as your dusting agent and becomes part of the final coating). Portion the mochi dough into 45g pieces. Work quickly while the mochi is still warm but cool enough to handle.

5

Fill & Wrap

Flatten each 45g piece into a round disc. Place a generous spoonful of the filling mixture in the centre. Gather the edges of the dough up and around the filling, pinching firmly to seal. Roll gently in the shredded coconut coating.

6

Set & Serve

Place completed mochi on a coconut-dusted tray. They can be served at room temperature or lightly chilled. Best eaten the day they are made, when the mochi is at its softest and most pillowy.

💡 Candy's Tips Work quickly once the mochi is cool enough to handle — it becomes stiffer as it cools further. If it tightens up, a brief moment back over the steamer will soften it again. Make sure your filling is ready before you start portioning so everything stays warm and pliable.

🛒 Outer Dough

  • Rice flour380g
  • Wheat starch70g
  • Sugar300g
  • Coconut milk (1 can)400g
  • Water300g
  • Oil70g
  • Food colouring2 drops

🍬 Filling

  • Coconut flakesto taste
  • Peanut candyto taste
  • Roasted white sesameto taste
  • Skor (toffee bits)to taste
  • Shredded coconut (coating)generous

📋 Quick Notes

Portion Size Each piece is 45g of dough. Use a kitchen scale for consistent results.

Storage Keep in an airtight container at room temperature for 1 day, or in the fridge for up to 2 days. Do not freeze.

Dietary Naturally dairy-free. Check your peanut candy and Skor labels for allergen info.

The Perfect Cookies

Loaded with oats, coconut, cranberries, blueberries, chocolate, toffee bits, and almonds — one cookie has it all.

About This Recipe

Some cookies are about one thing — chocolate, or caramel, or a single perfect ingredient shining through. These cookies are about everything. Oats for chew, coconut for richness, cranberries for tartness, and toasted almonds for texture — all held together with brown butter and a touch of vanilla.

The dough comes together quickly in one bowl — no chilling required — and the cookies bake in just 10 minutes. The key is pulling them while the centres still look slightly underdone. They firm up as they cool and end up with that perfect chewy-but-crispy texture.

Finish each one with a whole almond pressed on top before baking. It's a small touch that makes them look as good as they taste.

15
Min Prep
16–20
Cookies
★★★
Easy
10
Min Bake

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Preheat & Prep

Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C). Line two large baking trays with parchment paper and set aside.

2

Cream Butter & Sugars

In a large bowl, beat together ½ cup softened butter, ¼ cup packed brown sugar, and ¼ cup granulated sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in 1 egg, then add 1 tbsp milk and 1 tsp vanilla extract. Mix until combined.

3

Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a separate bowl, whisk together 1 cup all-purpose flour, ½ tsp baking powder, ½ tsp baking soda, and a pinch of salt. Stir in 1 cup large-flake rolled oats. Add the flour-oat mixture to the wet ingredients and mix until just combined.

4

Fold in the Good Stuff

Stir in ½ cup shredded coconut, ½ cup sliced almonds, ½ cup dried cranberries, ½ cup dried blueberries, ½ cup semi-sweet chocolate chips, ¼ cup SKOR toffee bits, and 2 tbsp flaxseeds. Mix gently until evenly distributed.

5

Scoop & Decorate

Use an ice-cream scoop to portion the dough onto the prepared trays, spacing each about 2 inches (5 cm) apart. Slightly press and flatten each ball. Press one whole almond into the top of each cookie for a decorative touch.

6

Bake & Cool

Bake for 10 minutes, until the edges are golden but the centres still look slightly underdone — this is correct. Let cool on the pans for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

💡 Candy's Tips Don't overbake these — the 10-minute mark is your friend. Pulling them early is the secret to that chewy, bakery-style texture. Room-temperature butter creams much more smoothly than cold butter.

🛒 Ingredients

  • Butter, softened½ cup
  • Brown sugar, packed¼ cup
  • Granulated sugar¼ cup
  • Egg1
  • Milk1 tbsp
  • Vanilla extract1 tsp
  • All-purpose flour1 cup
  • Large-flake rolled oats1 cup
  • Baking powder½ tsp
  • Baking soda½ tsp
  • Saltpinch
  • Large shredded coconut½ cup
  • Sliced almonds½ cup
  • Dried cranberries½ cup
  • Dried blueberries½ cup
  • Semi-sweet chocolate chips½ cup
  • SKOR toffee bits¼ cup
  • Flaxseeds2 tbsp
  • Whole almonds (for décor)16–20

📋 Quick Notes

Storage Store cooled cookies in an airtight container at room temp for 5 days. Freeze baked cookies or dough balls for up to 3 months.

Swap the Mix-Ins Swap dried blueberries for raisins, or use pecans instead of almonds. White chocolate chips work beautifully in place of semi-sweet.

Oven Tip Every oven runs differently — start checking at 9 minutes. The cookies should look underdone in the centre; they firm up as they cool.

Banana Cupcakes

Moist, warmly spiced banana cupcakes loaded with an incredible crunchy topping — coconut, chocolate, cranberries, almonds, and more.

About This Recipe

These banana cupcakes are everything you want from a banana bake — deeply moist, warmly spiced with cinnamon, and carrying that irresistible ripe banana sweetness. The sour cream keeps them tender and adds a subtle tang that balances the sweetness.

But the real magic is the topping. Instead of frosting, these cupcakes get an extraordinary crown of coconut flakes, chocolate chips, dried cranberries, flax seed, and sliced almonds. Every bite is a different texture and flavour.

The riper your bananas, the better. Look for ones with black-speckled skin — they're sweeter, softer, and pack far more banana flavour into the batter. If your bananas aren't quite there yet, keep them on the counter for a day.

15
Min Prep
12
Cupcakes
★★★
Easy

Min Bake

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Preheat & Prep

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a 12-hole muffin tin with paper cupcake cases or grease well. Peel 3 very ripe bananas and mash them thoroughly in a large bowl until smooth.

2

Mix the Wet Ingredients

To the mashed bananas, add ¾ cup sugar, ⅓ cup oil, 2 eggs, ½ cup sour cream, and 1 teaspoon vanilla extract. Whisk everything together until well combined and smooth. The batter should look creamy and cohesive.

3

Combine the Dry Ingredients

In a separate bowl, whisk together 1½ cups all-purpose flour, 1 tablespoon cinnamon, 1 teaspoon baking soda, and ¼ teaspoon salt. The generous amount of cinnamon is intentional — it adds warmth and depth without overpowering the banana.

4

Fold Together

Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and fold gently with a spatula until just combined — a few streaks of flour are fine. Do not overmix, as this will make the cupcakes tough.

5

Fill & Top

Divide the batter evenly between the 12 cupcake cases, filling each about ¾ full. Now comes the fun part: generously scatter the topping over each cupcake — ¼ cup coconut flakes, ½ cup chocolate chips, ½ cup dried cranberries, 2 tbsp flax seed, 1 tbsp Skor bits, and ½ cup sliced almonds divided among all cupcakes.

6

Bake

Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 30 minutes, until a skewer inserted in the centre comes out clean and the tops are golden. The coconut and almonds should be toasted and fragrant. Let cool in the tin for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack.

💡 Candy's Tips Use the blackest, most over-ripe bananas you can find — they make the most flavourful batter. The topping combination is generous by design; the different textures and flavours all balance each other beautifully. Don't skimp on the toppings.

🛒 Wet Ingredients

  • Very ripe bananas3
  • Sugar¾ cup
  • Oil⅓ cup
  • Eggs2
  • Sour cream½ cup
  • Vanilla extract1 tsp

🌾 Dry Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour1½ cups
  • Cinnamon1 tbsp
  • Baking soda1 tsp
  • Salt¼ tsp

🍫 Topping

  • Coconut flakes¼ cup
  • Chocolate chips½ cup
  • Dried cranberries½ cup
  • Flax seed2 tbsp
  • Skor (toffee bits)1 tbsp
  • Sliced almonds½ cup
  • Walnuts or extra choc chipsoptional

📋 Quick Notes

Storage Store in an airtight container at room temp for 3 days, or freeze individually for up to 2 months.

No Sour Cream? Full-fat Greek yoghurt is a perfect 1:1 substitute.

Temperature 350°F / 175°C for 30 minutes. Start checking at 25 minutes as ovens vary.

Mango Pudding

Silky, bright, and bursting with mango — this simple gelatin pudding with whipping cream and a squeeze of lemon is pure tropical comfort.

About This Recipe

Mango pudding holds a very special place in my heart. Every trip to yum cha (dim sum) with my family ended the same way — a wobbling pot of golden mango pudding shared between everyone at the table.

This version uses canned mango purée for consistent, intense flavour year-round, combined with whipping cream for richness and small chunks of fresh mango folded in for texture. A squeeze of lemon at the end brightens everything up perfectly.

The method is simple: bloom your gelatin, dissolve sugar in water, bring it all together, then chill. The hardest part really is waiting. Make it the night before a dinner party and you'll look like a hero.

20
Min Active
6
Servings
★★★
Easy
4h+
Chill Time

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Bloom the Gelatin

In a small bowl, sprinkle 6 tablespoons of gelatin powder over 1 cup of cold water. Stir briefly, then let sit for 5–10 minutes without disturbing until it becomes spongy and swollen. This is the blooming process.

2

Make the Sugar Syrup

In a medium saucepan, combine 4 cups of water with 1 cup of sugar. Bring to a gentle heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves completely. Do not boil vigorously. Once the sugar is fully dissolved, remove from heat.

3

Dissolve the Gelatin

Add the bloomed gelatin to the warm sugar syrup and stir gently until completely dissolved and the liquid is clear with no lumps. Remove from heat and allow to cool for a few minutes until it's warm but not hot.

4

Add Mango Purée

Stir in the full can of mango purée until completely combined. The mixture should turn a vivid golden-orange. Taste at this point — it should be intensely mango-flavoured and not overly sweet.

5

Fold in Mango Bits & Cream

Stir in 1 cup of whipping cream and gently fold in small diced fresh mango pieces for texture. Mix until uniform. Finally, squeeze in the juice of half a lemon and stir — this brightens the flavour beautifully.

6

Pour, Set & Serve

Pour the mixture through a fine sieve into individual ramekins, glasses, or moulds. Tap gently on the counter to release air bubbles. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, until fully set.

💡 Candy's Tips The order matters here: water → sugar → gelatin → mango purée → mango pieces → whipping cream → lemon. Adding the cream and lemon last keeps the pudding from curdling. Don't skip the sieve step — it ensures a perfectly smooth, elegant pudding.

🛒 Ingredients

  • Canned mango purée1 can
  • Whipping cream1 cup
  • Gelatin powder6 tbsp
  • Fresh mango (diced small)to taste
  • Water (for gelatin)1 cup
  • Water (for syrup)4 cups
  • Sugar1 cup
  • Lemon½ (juiced)

📋 Quick Notes

Storage Keep covered in the fridge for up to 3 days. The texture is best in the first 48 hours.

Make-Ahead Perfect for dinner parties — make the day before and refrigerate overnight for best results.

Serving Tip Serve cold, drizzled with a little extra whipping cream or condensed milk on top.

Chinese Egg Tarts

Silky, lightly sweet custard nestled in a golden pastry shell — a timeless classic straight from the dim sum cart to your kitchen.

About This Recipe

Chinese egg tarts — or dàn tāt — are one of those recipes that carry so much memory in a single bite. A crumbly, golden pastry shell cradling a perfectly smooth, barely-sweet egg custard. Iconic. Essential. Life-changing.

This is my simple, home-friendly version that gets you there without a pastry degree. The custard itself is beautifully straightforward: sugar, a touch of condensed milk for a gentle caramel sweetness, and eggs.

The key to perfect egg tarts is patience — gentle heat to set the custard without bubbling, and a proper resting time after baking so everything firms up into that classic smooth, glossy finish.

10
Min Prep
8
Large Tarts
★★★
Easy
25
Min Bake

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Preheat Your Oven

Preheat your oven to 350°C. Set your egg tart moulds on a baking tray and have them ready to fill. Make sure your pastry shells are sitting snugly in the moulds before you pour the custard.

2

Dissolve the Sugar

In a large bowl or jug, combine 150g of sugar with 250g of lukewarm water. Stir well until the sugar is completely dissolved — this is important, as undissolved sugar can cause grittiness in the final custard.

3

Mix in Condensed Milk & Eggs

Stir in 1 heaped tablespoon of condensed milk until fully combined. In a separate bowl, lightly beat 3 eggs, then gently stir them into the sugar mixture. Avoid whisking vigorously — you want to incorporate the eggs without creating too much foam.

4

Strain the Custard

Pour the entire custard mixture through a fine mesh sieve into a clean jug. This removes any egg threads or lumps and is the single most important step for achieving that perfect silky texture.

5

Fill the Tart Shells

Carefully pour the strained custard into each pastry shell, filling to about 80–85% full — the custard will puff very slightly as it bakes. Pour slowly and steadily to avoid creating bubbles.

6

Bake & Rest

Carefully slide the tray into the oven and bake for 25 minutes, until the custard is just set with only the faintest wobble in the very centre and the pastry is golden at the edges. Cool completely in the moulds — do not unmould while hot.

💡 Candy's Tips Straining the custard and resting after baking are the two steps you must never skip — they're what separates a great egg tart from a good one. Lukewarm water (not hot) keeps the eggs from scrambling as you mix.

🛒 Ingredients

  • Sugar150g
  • Lukewarm water250g
  • Condensed milk1 tbsp
  • Eggs, strained3
  • Egg tart pastry shells8 large

📋 Quick Notes

Storage Best enjoyed fresh and warm. Store leftovers covered in the fridge for up to 2 days. Reheat in a low oven at 150°C for 5–8 minutes to revive the pastry.

Pastry Shells Ready-made egg tart shells are available at most Asian grocery stores and save a lot of time. Homemade shortcrust or flaky pastry shells also work beautifully.

No Bubbles Pour slowly, don't whisk, and pop any surface bubbles with a toothpick before baking for the smoothest finish.
