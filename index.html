Coconut Mochi

Pillowy, chewy steamed mochi filled with coconut flakes, peanut candy, roasted sesame, and Skor — wrapped in shredded coconut for a truly indulgent bite.

About This Recipe

This coconut mochi is a labour of love — and one of the most satisfying things to make in my kitchen. The outer dough is steamed rather than microwaved, giving it a deeply chewy, silky texture that stores beautifully.

The filling is what makes this truly special. A combination of toasted coconut flakes, crunchy peanut candy, nutty roasted sesame, and the buttery toffee crunch of Skor creates a sweet symphony in every bite.

This recipe takes about an hour of steaming, but the active work is minimal. Make it on a weekend afternoon, and you'll have a stunning box of mochi ready to share — or keep all to yourself.

20
Min Prep
~16
Pieces
★★★
Easy
60
Min Steam

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Mix the Mochi Dough

In a large bowl, combine 380g rice flour, 70g wheat starch, 300g sugar, one 400g can of full-fat coconut milk, 300g water, 70g oil, and 2 drops of food colouring. Whisk until smooth.

2

Steam the Dough

Pour into a greased tray, cover with foil, and steam for 60 minutes until cooked through and translucent.

3

Prepare the Filling

Toast coconut flakes, crush peanut candy, and mix with roasted sesame and Skor bits.

4

Portion the Dough

Dust work surface with shredded coconut and portion dough into 45g pieces while still warm.

5

Fill & Wrap

Flatten each piece, add filling, gather edges to seal, and roll in shredded coconut.

6

Set & Serve

Place on coconut-dusted tray. Serve at room temperature or chilled. Best eaten fresh.

💡 Candy's Tips Work quickly once mochi cools — it stiffens further. If needed, reheat briefly over steamer to soften. Have filling ready before portioning.

🛒 Outer Dough

  • Rice flour380g
  • Wheat starch70g
  • Sugar300g
  • Coconut milk400g
  • Water300g
  • Oil70g
  • Food colouring2 drops

🍬 Filling

  • Coconut flakesto taste
  • Peanut candyto taste
  • Roasted sesameto taste
  • Skor toffee bitsto taste
  • Shredded coconutgenerous

📋 Quick Notes

Portion Size Each piece is 45g. Use a scale for consistency.

Storage Room temperature 1 day, or refrigerated up to 2 days. Do not freeze.

Dietary Naturally dairy-free.

The Perfect Cookies

Loaded with oats, coconut, cranberries, blueberries, chocolate, toffee bits, and almonds — one cookie has it all.

About This Recipe

These cookies are about everything. Oats for chew, coconut for richness, cranberries for tartness, and almonds for texture — held together with brown butter and vanilla.

The dough comes together in one bowl with no chilling needed, and bakes in just 10 minutes. Pull them while the centres look slightly underdone for perfect chewy-crispy texture.

Finish with a whole almond on top before baking for a beautiful touch that tastes as good as it looks.

15
Min Prep
16–20
Cookies
★★★
Easy
10
Min Bake

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Preheat & Prep

Preheat oven to 350°F. Line two baking trays with parchment paper.

2

Cream Butter & Sugars

Beat ½ cup softened butter with ¼ cup brown sugar and ¼ cup granulated sugar until fluffy. Add 1 egg, 1 tbsp milk, and 1 tsp vanilla.

3

Mix Dry Ingredients

Whisk 1 cup flour, ½ tsp baking powder, ½ tsp baking soda, and salt. Stir in 1 cup oats and add to wet mix.

4

Fold in Mix-Ins

Stir in coconut, almonds, cranberries, blueberries, chocolate chips, Skor bits, and flaxseeds.

5

Scoop & Decorate

Portion onto trays 2 inches apart. Press one whole almond on each cookie.

6

Bake & Cool

Bake 10 minutes until edges are golden. Cool on pan 5 minutes, then transfer to rack.

💡 Candy's Tips Don't overbake — 10 minutes is key for chewy texture. Pull early for the best result. Use room-temperature butter for smooth creaming.

🛒 Ingredients

  • Butter, softened½ cup
  • Brown sugar¼ cup
  • Granulated sugar¼ cup
  • Egg1
  • Milk1 tbsp
  • Vanilla extract1 tsp
  • All-purpose flour1 cup
  • Large-flake oats1 cup
  • Baking powder½ tsp
  • Baking soda½ tsp
  • Saltpinch
  • Shredded coconut½ cup
  • Sliced almonds½ cup
  • Dried cranberries½ cup
  • Dried blueberries½ cup
  • Chocolate chips½ cup
  • Skor toffee bits¼ cup
  • Flaxseeds2 tbsp
  • Whole almonds16–20

📋 Quick Notes

Storage Room temp 5 days in airtight container. Freeze for up to 3 months.

Substitutions Use raisins for blueberries, pecans for almonds, or white chocolate for semi-sweet.

Oven Tip Start checking at 9 minutes. Cookies should look slightly underdone in centre.

Banana Cupcakes

Moist, warmly spiced banana cupcakes loaded with an incredible crunchy topping — coconut, chocolate, cranberries, almonds, and more.

About This Recipe

These banana cupcakes are deeply moist, warmly spiced with cinnamon, and carry irresistible ripe banana sweetness. Sour cream keeps them tender with a subtle tang.

Instead of frosting, they get an extraordinary crown of coconut, chocolate, cranberries, flax seed, and almonds. Every bite is a different texture.

Use black-speckled bananas — they're sweeter and pack more flavour. Keep on counter another day if not quite ripe enough.

15
Min Prep
12
Cupcakes
★★★
Easy
30
Min Bake

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Preheat & Prep

Preheat to 350°F. Line 12-hole muffin tin. Mash 3 very ripe bananas thoroughly.

2

Mix Wet Ingredients

Add ¾ cup sugar, ⅓ cup oil, 2 eggs, ½ cup sour cream, and 1 tsp vanilla to bananas. Whisk until smooth.

3

Combine Dry Ingredients

Whisk 1½ cups flour, 1 tbsp cinnamon, 1 tsp baking soda, and ¼ tsp salt.

4

Fold Together

Add dry to wet and fold gently with spatula until just combined. A few flour streaks are fine.

5

Fill & Top

Divide batter evenly. Generously scatter topping: coconut, chocolate chips, cranberries, flax, Skor, almonds.

6

Bake

Bake 30 minutes at 350°F until golden and skewer comes out clean. Cool in tin 5 minutes.

💡 Candy's Tips Use the blackest, most over-ripe bananas. The topping is generous by design — don't skimp! All the textures balance beautifully.

🛒 Wet Ingredients

  • Very ripe bananas3
  • Sugar¾ cup
  • Oil⅓ cup
  • Eggs2
  • Sour cream½ cup
  • Vanilla extract1 tsp

🌾 Dry Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour1½ cups
  • Cinnamon1 tbsp
  • Baking soda1 tsp
  • Salt¼ tsp

🍫 Topping

  • Coconut flakes¼ cup
  • Chocolate chips½ cup
  • Dried cranberries½ cup
  • Flax seed2 tbsp
  • Skor toffee bits1 tbsp
  • Sliced almonds½ cup

📋 Quick Notes

Storage Room temp 3 days, or freeze individually up to 2 months.

No Sour Cream? Full-fat Greek yoghurt works 1:1.

Temperature 350°F for 30 minutes. Check at 25 minutes.

Mango Pudding

Silky, bright, and bursting with mango — this simple gelatin pudding with whipping cream and a squeeze of lemon is pure tropical comfort.

About This Recipe

Mango pudding holds special place in my heart. Every dim sum trip ended with a wobbling pot shared between everyone.

This uses canned mango purée for consistent year-round flavour, combined with whipping cream for richness and fresh mango chunks for texture. Lemon juice brightens perfectly.

Simple method: bloom gelatin, dissolve sugar, mix together, chill. Hardest part is waiting. Make the night before for dinner parties.

20
Min Active
6
Servings
★★★
Easy
4h+
Chill

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Bloom Gelatin

Sprinkle 6 tbsp gelatin over 1 cup cold water. Let sit 5–10 minutes until spongy and swollen.

2

Make Sugar Syrup

Combine 4 cups water with 1 cup sugar. Heat gently, stirring until dissolved. Don't boil hard.

3

Dissolve Gelatin

Add bloomed gelatin to warm syrup. Stir until clear with no lumps. Cool a few minutes.

4

Add Mango Purée

Stir in full can of mango purée until combined. Should be vivid golden-orange and intensely flavoured.

5

Fold in Cream & Mango

Stir in 1 cup whipping cream and fold in diced fresh mango. Squeeze lemon juice in for brightness.

6

Pour, Set, Serve

Pour through fine sieve into ramekins. Chill 4+ hours or overnight until fully set.

💡 Candy's Tips Order matters: water → sugar → gelatin → mango → mango bits → cream → lemon. Adding cream and lemon last prevents curdling. Don't skip the sieve for smoothness.

🛒 Ingredients

  • Canned mango purée1 can
  • Whipping cream1 cup
  • Gelatin powder6 tbsp
  • Fresh mango (diced)to taste
  • Water (gelatin)1 cup
  • Water (syrup)4 cups
  • Sugar1 cup
  • Lemon½ (juiced)

📋 Quick Notes

Storage Covered in fridge up to 3 days. Best in first 48 hours.

Make-Ahead Perfect for parties — make the day before overnight.

Serving Serve cold with extra whipping cream or condensed milk drizzle.

Chinese Egg Tarts

Silky, lightly sweet custard nestled in a golden pastry shell — a timeless classic straight from the dim sum cart to your kitchen.

About This Recipe

Chinese egg tarts are one of those recipes carrying so much memory. A crumbly golden pastry shell cradling perfectly smooth, barely-sweet egg custard.

This simple home-friendly version doesn't need pastry skills. Straightforward custard: sugar, condensed milk for gentle caramel sweetness, and eggs.

Key to perfect tarts: gentle heat to set custard without bubbling, and proper resting after baking so it firms into classic smooth, glossy finish.

10
Min Prep
8
Large Tarts
★★★
Easy
25
Min Bake

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Preheat Oven

Preheat to 350°C. Set egg tart moulds on tray. Ensure pastry shells sit snugly.

2

Dissolve Sugar

Combine 150g sugar with 250g lukewarm water. Stir until fully dissolved — undissolved sugar causes grittiness.

3

Mix Condensed Milk & Eggs

Stir in 1 tbsp condensed milk. Lightly beat 3 eggs separately, then gently stir into mixture. Avoid vigorous whisking.

4

Strain Custard

Pour through fine mesh sieve into clean jug. Removes egg threads/lumps — essential for silky texture.

5

Fill Shells

Carefully pour strained custard into shells, filling 80–85% full. Pour slowly to avoid bubbles.

6

Bake & Rest

Bake 25 minutes until custard sets with faint wobble in centre and pastry is golden. Cool in moulds.

💡 Candy's Tips Never skip straining and resting — these separate great tarts from good ones. Lukewarm water (not hot) prevents eggs from scrambling during mixing.

🛒 Ingredients

  • Sugar150g
  • Lukewarm water250g
  • Condensed milk1 tbsp
  • Eggs3
  • Egg tart shells8 large

📋 Quick Notes

Storage Best fresh and warm. Fridge up to 2 days. Reheat at 150°C for 5–8 minutes.

Pastry Shells Ready-made shells available at Asian stores save time. Homemade shortcrust works too.

No Bubbles Pour slowly, don't whisk. Pop surface bubbles with toothpick before baking.
