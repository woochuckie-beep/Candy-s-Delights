Bakery-Style
Blueberry Muffins

Impossibly tall domes, juicy blueberries in every bite, and a sparkling sugar crust that crackles just right.

🧁

About This Recipe

There's something deeply satisfying about a proper bakery-style muffin — tall, domed, golden on top, and stuffed with fruit. For years, my home muffins were flat and dense, nothing like the ones in the café window. Then I discovered three things that changed everything: a hot oven start, sour cream in the batter, and overfilling the tins.

The hot oven blast (220°C for the first 5 minutes) creates a dramatic rise before the structure sets, giving you that sky-high dome. Sour cream adds tenderness and just the right tang to balance the sweetness. And yes — fill those cups all the way to the top. Don't be shy.

These muffins are best eaten warm, about 10 minutes out of the oven. But they're also brilliant the next morning, split and toasted with a little butter. I've been making this recipe for three years and it never gets old.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Prep & Preheat

Preheat your oven to 220°C (200°C fan). Line a 12-hole muffin tin with paper cases (or grease well). Toss 250g fresh or frozen blueberries with 1 tbsp plain flour — this prevents them from sinking to the bottom. Set aside.

2

Make the Wet Mix

In a large bowl, whisk together 2 large eggs, 200g caster sugar, 80ml vegetable oil, and 120ml full-fat sour cream until smooth and pale. Add 1 tsp vanilla extract and the zest of 1 lemon. Whisk again briefly. The sour cream is non-negotiable — it's the key to tenderness and flavour.

3

Combine Dry Ingredients

In a separate bowl, whisk 300g plain flour, 2 tsp baking powder, ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda, and ½ tsp fine salt. Pour the wet mixture into the dry ingredients and fold with a spatula until just combined — lumps are fine and even good. Overmixing develops gluten and makes tough muffins. Stop while you still see some streaks.

4

Fold in Blueberries

Gently fold the flour-coated blueberries into the batter with 3–4 large folds. Some will burst and streak the batter purple — that's beautiful, not a mistake. The batter will be quite thick, which is correct.

5

Fill & Top

Divide the batter between the 12 muffin cases, filling them all the way to the very top (this is essential for tall domes). Mix 2 tbsp granulated sugar with ½ tsp cinnamon and sprinkle generously over the top of each muffin. Place a few extra blueberries on top for effect.

6

The Hot-Start Bake

Bake at 220°C for 5 minutes, then reduce to 180°C (160°C fan) without opening the door. Bake for a further 15–17 minutes until a skewer inserted in the centre comes out clean and the tops are deep golden. Rest in the tin for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack. Try to wait at least 10 minutes before eating (I know — it's hard).

💡 Candy's Tips The two-temperature bake is the secret to tall domes — don't skip it. If using frozen blueberries, don't thaw them first. Room temperature eggs and sour cream blend much more smoothly than cold ones. For extra bakery vibes, add a small cube of cream cheese into the centre of each muffin before baking.

📋 Quick Notes

Storage Store in an airtight container at room temp for 3 days, or freeze individually for up to 2 months.

Variations Swap blueberries for raspberries, blackberries, or chopped peaches. Add white chocolate chips for extra indulgence.

No Sour Cream? Full-fat Greek yoghurt works as a 1:1 substitute and gives similar results.
