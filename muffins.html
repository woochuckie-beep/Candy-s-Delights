Banana Cupcakes

Moist, warmly spiced banana cupcakes loaded with an incredible crunchy topping — coconut, chocolate, cranberries, almonds, and more.

🧁

About This Recipe

These banana cupcakes are everything you want from a banana bake — deeply moist, warmly spiced with cinnamon, and carrying that irresistible ripe banana sweetness. The sour cream keeps the crumb tender and adds a subtle tang that balances the sweetness beautifully.

But the real magic is the topping. Instead of frosting, these cupcakes get an extraordinary crown of coconut flakes, chocolate chips, dried cranberries, flax seed, and sliced almonds — all pressed gently on top of the batter before baking. It toasts as the cupcakes bake, creating a crunchy, nutty, chocolatey crust that is genuinely addictive.

The riper your bananas, the better. Look for ones with black-speckled skin — they're sweeter, softer, and pack far more banana flavour into the batter. If your bananas aren't quite there, you can speed-ripen them in a 150°C oven for 15 minutes.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Preheat & Prep

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a 12-hole muffin tin with paper cupcake cases or grease well. Peel 3 very ripe bananas and mash them thoroughly in a large bowl until smooth with only a few small lumps remaining.

2

Mix the Wet Ingredients

To the mashed bananas, add ¾ cup sugar, ⅓ cup oil, 2 eggs, ½ cup sour cream, and 1 teaspoon vanilla extract. Whisk everything together until well combined and smooth. The batter will be thick and fragrant.

3

Combine the Dry Ingredients

In a separate bowl, whisk together 1½ cups all-purpose flour, 1 tablespoon cinnamon, 1 teaspoon baking soda, and ¼ teaspoon salt. The generous amount of cinnamon is intentional — it pairs beautifully with banana and makes the whole kitchen smell incredible.

4

Fold Together

Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and fold gently with a spatula until just combined — a few streaks of flour are fine. Do not overmix, as this will make the cupcakes tough. The batter should be thick and spoonable.

5

Fill & Top

Divide the batter evenly between the 12 cupcake cases, filling each about ¾ full. Now comes the fun part: generously scatter the topping over each cupcake — ¼ cup coconut flakes, ½ cup chocolate chips, ½ cup dried cranberries, 2 tablespoons flax seed, 1 tablespoon Skor (toffee bits), and ½ cup sliced almonds, divided between all 12. Press lightly so the toppings adhere. Add a few extra walnuts or chocolate chips on the very top if desired.

6

Bake

Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 30 minutes, until a skewer inserted in the centre comes out clean and the tops are golden. The coconut and almonds should be toasted and fragrant. Let cool in the tin for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack. Best enjoyed warm or at room temperature.

💡 Candy's Tips Use the blackest, most over-ripe bananas you can find — they make the most flavourful batter. The topping combination is generous by design; the different textures and flavours all bake together into something magical. Don't be shy with it. If you prefer a tidier look, push the toppings slightly into the batter before baking so they stay in place.

🛒 Wet Ingredients

  • Very ripe bananas3
  • Sugar¾ cup
  • Oil⅓ cup
  • Eggs2
  • Sour cream½ cup
  • Vanilla extract1 tsp

🌾 Dry Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour1½ cups
  • Cinnamon1 tbsp
  • Baking soda1 tsp
  • Salt¼ tsp

🍫 Topping

  • Coconut flakes¼ cup
  • Chocolate chips½ cup
  • Dried cranberries½ cup
  • Flax seed2 tbsp
  • Skor (toffee bits)1 tbsp
  • Sliced almonds½ cup
  • Walnuts or extra choc chipsoptional

📋 Quick Notes

Storage Store in an airtight container at room temp for 3 days, or freeze individually for up to 2 months.

No Sour Cream? Full-fat Greek yoghurt is a perfect 1:1 substitute.

Temperature 350°F / 175°C for 30 minutes. Start checking at 25 minutes as ovens vary.
