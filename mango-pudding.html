Hong Kong
Mango Pudding

Silky smooth, intensely mango, set just perfectly — a classic dim sum dessert that belongs in every home kitchen.

🍮

About This Recipe

Mango pudding holds a very special place in my heart. Every trip to yum cha (dim sum) with my family ended the same way — a wobbling pot of golden mango pudding, drizzled with evaporated milk, shared between everyone at the table. It was always the highlight.

For years I thought it must be complicated to recreate at home. As it turns out, it's one of the simplest desserts I know. Just five core ingredients, no baking required, and a mere 20 minutes of active time. The hardest part is waiting for it to set in the fridge.

The key to a truly excellent mango pudding is ripe, fragrant mango. Fresh Alphonso mangoes in season are extraordinary; outside of mango season, a good quality frozen mango purée works beautifully. Don't use out-of-season supermarket mangoes — the flavour just isn't there. This recipe is simple enough that quality ingredients make all the difference.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Bloom the Gelatine

In a small bowl, sprinkle 2½ tsp (8g) unflavoured gelatine powder over 60ml cold water. Let it sit without stirring for 5–10 minutes until it becomes spongy and swells. This "blooming" step is essential — it ensures the gelatine dissolves smoothly without lumps.

2

Warm the Mixture

In a small saucepan, warm 80ml full-fat coconut milk and 60g caster sugar over low-medium heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves completely. Do not boil. Remove from heat and add the bloomed gelatine, stirring until completely dissolved and the liquid is clear. Set aside to cool for 5 minutes.

3

Blend the Mango

Blend 500g very ripe mango flesh (about 2–3 large mangoes, or frozen mango purée) until completely smooth. You should have about 400ml of thick, vibrant purée. Taste — it should be intensely mango-forward and sweet. If the mango isn't sweet enough, add an extra tablespoon of sugar to the warm gelatine mixture.

4

Combine Everything

Stir the cooled gelatine mixture into the mango purée along with 200ml evaporated milk (or double cream for a richer version). Mix until completely smooth and uniform. The colour should be a vivid, deep golden yellow. Taste once more — adjust sweetness if needed.

5

Pour & Set

Pour the mixture through a fine sieve into a jug for easy pouring, then divide between 6 individual ramekins, pudding moulds, or glasses. Tap gently on the counter to release air bubbles. Cover each one with plastic wrap and refrigerate for a minimum of 4 hours, ideally overnight.

6

Serve & Garnish

Serve straight in the ramekins, or unmould onto plates: run a thin knife around the edge, place a plate on top, and flip quickly. Drizzle generously with evaporated milk or single cream. Garnish with diced fresh mango, a sprig of mint, and if you can find it, a tiny pinch of Tajín or Chamoy for a contemporary twist.

💡 Candy's Tips The quality of your mango is everything in this recipe — use the ripest, most fragrant mangoes you can find. Alphonso, Ataulfo, and Honey mangoes are all excellent choices. If you want an extra silky texture, strain the purée through a fine sieve before blending with the gelatine. For a beautiful presentation, set the pudding in a round ramekin and unmould for that classic Hong Kong style dome.

📋 Quick Notes

Storage Keep covered in the fridge for up to 3 days. The texture is best in the first 48 hours.

Dairy-Free Version Replace evaporated milk with full-fat coconut cream for a tropical dairy-free version.

Agar-Agar Version For a firmer, vegan-friendly pudding, use 2 tsp agar-agar powder instead of gelatine. Boil with the coconut milk.

Make-Ahead This pudding improves overnight. Perfect for dinner parties — make the day before and refrigerate.
