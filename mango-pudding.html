Mango Pudding

Silky, bright, and bursting with mango — this simple gelatin pudding with whipping cream and a squeeze of lemon is pure tropical comfort.

About This Recipe

Mango pudding holds a very special place in my heart. Every trip to yum cha (dim sum) with my family ended the same way — a wobbling pot of golden mango pudding shared between everyone at the table. It was always the highlight.

This version uses canned mango purée for consistent, intense flavour year-round, combined with whipping cream for richness and small chunks of fresh mango folded in for texture. A squeeze of lemon at the end brightens the whole thing and makes the mango flavour really sing.

The method is simple: bloom your gelatin, dissolve sugar in water, bring it all together, then chill. The hardest part really is waiting. Make it the night before a dinner party and you'll be rewarded with the most impressive, effortless dessert on the table.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Bloom the Gelatin

In a small bowl, sprinkle 6 tablespoons of gelatin powder over 1 cup of cold water. Stir briefly, then let sit for 5–10 minutes without disturbing until it becomes spongy and swollen. This blooming step ensures the gelatin will dissolve completely without any lumps.

2

Make the Sugar Syrup

In a medium saucepan, combine 4 cups of water with 1 cup of sugar. Bring to a gentle heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves completely. Do not boil vigorously. Once the sugar is fully dissolved, reduce the heat to low.

3

Dissolve the Gelatin

Add the bloomed gelatin to the warm sugar syrup and stir gently until completely dissolved and the liquid is clear with no lumps. Remove from heat and allow to cool for a few minutes — it should be warm but not steaming hot before you add the other ingredients.

4

Add Mango Purée

Stir in the full can of mango purée until completely combined. The mixture should turn a vivid golden-orange. Taste at this point — it should be intensely mango-flavoured and well-sweetened. Adjust sugar if your purée is less sweet.

5

Fold in Mango Bits & Cream

Stir in 1 cup of whipping cream and gently fold in small diced fresh mango pieces for texture. Mix until uniform. Finally, squeeze in the juice of half a lemon and stir — this is the secret finishing touch that lifts and brightens the whole pudding.

6

Pour, Set & Serve

Pour the mixture through a fine sieve into individual ramekins, glasses, or moulds. Tap gently on the counter to release air bubbles. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for a minimum of 4 hours, or overnight. Serve straight in the ramekins or unmould onto plates for a classic dome presentation.

💡 Candy's Tips The order matters here: water → sugar → gelatin → mango purée → mango pieces → whipping cream → lemon. Adding the cream and lemon last keeps the pudding from curdling. Don't skip the lemon — even a small amount makes the flavour dramatically brighter. Use the best quality canned mango purée you can find; Alphonso mango purée is exceptional.

🛒 Ingredients

  • Canned mango purée1 can
  • Whipping cream1 cup
  • Gelatin powder6 tbsp
  • Fresh mango (diced small)to taste
  • Water (for gelatin)1 cup
  • Water (for syrup)4 cups
  • Sugar1 cup
  • Lemon½ (juiced)

📋 Quick Notes

Storage Keep covered in the fridge for up to 3 days. The texture is best in the first 48 hours.

Make-Ahead Perfect for dinner parties — make the day before and refrigerate overnight for best results.

Serving Tip Serve cold, drizzled with a little extra whipping cream or condensed milk on top.
