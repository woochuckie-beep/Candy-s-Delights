Brown Butter Chocolate Chip Cookies

The only chocolate chip cookie recipe you'll ever need — deeply nutty, perfectly chewy, and impossibly good.

🍪

About This Recipe

I've made hundreds of batches of chocolate chip cookies over the years, tweaking ratios, temperatures, and techniques until I landed on this version — which I genuinely believe is the best cookie I've ever tasted. The secret? Brown butter.

Browning the butter removes the water content and toasts the milk solids, creating a deep, nutty, almost caramel-like flavour that elevates a classic cookie into something extraordinary. Combined with a rest in the fridge, high-quality chocolate, and the right ratio of brown to white sugar, these cookies are everything a cookie should be.

They're crispy at the edges, pillowy-soft in the centre, and packed with chocolate in every bite. This is my most-requested recipe, and now it's yours.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Brown the Butter

Melt 225g unsalted butter in a light-coloured saucepan over medium heat, stirring constantly. As it melts, it will foam, then subside, then foam again. When it turns a deep amber and smells nutty (about 5–7 minutes), remove from heat and pour into a large mixing bowl. Let it cool for 15 minutes — it should be warm but not hot.

2

Mix the Sugars

Add 200g dark brown sugar and 100g caster sugar to the cooled brown butter. Whisk vigorously for 2 minutes until the mixture is thick and glossy. The sugar should mostly dissolve. This step aerates the mixture and develops flavour.

3

Add Eggs & Vanilla

Add 2 large eggs and 1 egg yolk, one at a time, whisking well after each. Add 2 tsp vanilla extract. The mixture should look thick, smooth, and slightly paler. Whisk for another minute to incorporate air.

4

Fold in Dry Ingredients

Sift in 280g plain flour, 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda, and 1 tsp fine salt. Using a spatula, fold until just combined — don't overmix. Fold in 250g good-quality chocolate chips or chopped dark chocolate (70% cocoa). Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 1 hour (or up to 72 hours — the longer, the better the flavour).

5

Preheat & Portion

Preheat your oven to 180°C (160°C fan) and line two large baking trays with parchment. Scoop the dough into 50g balls (about 2 tablespoons each) and place them 6cm apart on the trays. Don't flatten — they'll spread as they bake. Press a few extra chocolate chips on top for that Pinterest-worthy look.

6

Bake to Perfection

Bake for 11–13 minutes until the edges are set and golden but the centres still look slightly underdone — they'll continue to cook on the tray. Immediately sprinkle with flaky sea salt. Bang the tray on the counter once to give them that crinkled top. Let cool on the tray for 5 minutes before transferring.

💡 Candy's Tips The single biggest game-changer is resting the dough — at least 1 hour, but ideally overnight. It hydrates the flour fully and deepens the flavour dramatically. Also: always use room-temperature eggs for a smoother, more emulsified dough. And please, please use good chocolate.

📋 Quick Notes

Storage Store cooled cookies in an airtight container at room temp for 5 days. Freeze dough balls for up to 3 months.

Make It Nutty Add 100g toasted walnuts or pecans for extra texture and flavour.

Oven Tip Every oven is different — start checking at 10 minutes. Underbaking slightly gives the best chewy result.
